02 · Anatomy of the pass

Front matters. Back delivers.

The front is what a member sees in the lock-screen flash. The back is where the scheme proves it's a living, breathing programme — not a printed loyalty card with a QR slapped on.

Front of pass

visible at a glance
  1. Strip image — Dartmouth, Salcombe, or a Dartmoor tor. Refreshed seasonally to keep the pass feeling alive.
  2. Visit South Devon logo — top-left, where members already look for brand identity in Apple Wallet.
  3. Member tier — "Resident Explorer", "Gold Member", "Founding Friend". A clear ladder to climb.
  4. Member name & pass ID — large for the human, small & monospaced for the database.
  5. Live deals counter — 4 / 10 redeemed, with a progress bar. This is the proof of life partner venues need to see.
  6. QR code — high-contrast, scannable from any merchant phone. Encodes the pass code, not personal data.

Back of pass

the evolution layer
  1. Integration link — "View all 150+ current offers at devondaysout.co.uk/my-deals."
  2. Seasonal spotlight — "20% off at Brixham Fish Market · ends 31 May." Updated centrally; appears on every member's pass instantly.
  3. Validation instructions — for the merchant: "Scan code to verify membership and log redemption."
  4. Privacy & support — partner contact, data policy link, revoke pass.
  5. Audit metadata — issued date, last updated, geofence regions enabled.

04 · The honest answer

"Can I send a push that pops up on the lock screen?"

Yes — but it's worth knowing how Apple Wallet actually behaves, because it's not quite the same as a regular app push. There are three ways Devon Days Out earns lock-screen real estate, and all three are demonstrable.

1. Geofenced relevance — for "Welcome to Dartmouth"

This is the cleanest answer to your example. The pass carries a list of locations and a relevantText. When the member's iPhone is near one of them, iOS surfaces the pass on the lock screen, with that text. No server push required — the device decides. Apple permits up to 10 locations per pass.

2. Server-pushed pass updates — for "new offer added"

Your dashboard updates a field (e.g., "Current Spotlight"). PassQR sends a silent APNs ping. iOS fetches the new JSON and shows a small banner: "Pass updated · 20% off at Brixham Fish Market". The text comes from the field's changeMessage template. It is not arbitrary marketing copy — it is the actual change, framed as a notification.

3. Google Wallet messages — more flexible on Android

Google Wallet exposes a messages array on the pass. You can post a free-form title + body and it appears as a banner on Android devices. Closer to "real" push, but still attached to the pass — not a separate app channel.

So can you push "Welcome to Dartmouth! Here are your local deals" automatically?

Yes — and the right mechanism is geofencing, not server push. It's better, because it fires the moment a member arrives in town without you having to know they did. For seasonal copy refreshes ("Spring → Summer deals"), use server push. The two work in concert.
